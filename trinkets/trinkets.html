Sofie's Favorite Trinkets

Kewpie Babies

Kewpie Babies originated from cartoonist Rosie O'Neill in 1909 in a comic strip. The name Kewpie was inspired by the God of Love, aka Cupid.

Kewpie became the mascot for Japan's first-ever mayonnaise, sold in Japan: Kewpie Mayo.

Monchichi

Monchichis originated in 1974 from the brand Sekiguchi Corporation. Monchichis were particularly popular during the 1980's.

The name Monchichi is a combination of the French word "Mon" meaning my and "chichi" which is the sound that babies make when they suck their pacifier in Japanese.

Vintage Trolls

Vintage trolls were created by a Danish man, Thomas Dam, in 1956. They rose in popularity from the 60s to the 90s. Many companies took inspiration from Thomas Dam and made similar trolls, such as Treasure trolls.

DreamWorks bought the company in 2013, which led to the creation of the Trolls movie franchise.

Tokidoki Figures

Tokidoki figures stemmed from the brand started by Simone Legno, who launched the brand in 2005. In Japanese, "tokidoki" means "sometimes". Tokidoki figures first started being released in 2008.
